The Philosophy

Restraint is the
rarest ingredient

Chef Élise Marchand cooks the way a poet edits — removing until only the essential remains. A single langoustine. Three drops of brown butter. Nothing on the plate that has not earned its place.

Her kitchen keeps no freezer and no shortcuts. Produce arrives each dawn from growers she has known for twenty years, and the menu is rewritten around whatever the morning brings.

“We do not decorate plates. We finish thoughts.”

Élise Marchand — Chef & Propriétaire

The Cuisine

The Cellar

Twelve metres
below the street

Beneath the dining room, 14,000 bottles rest in seventeenth-century stone. Sommelier Théo Lenoir walks the racks each afternoon, matching the cellar's memory to the evening's menu.

The Classic Pairing

Seven pours drawn from Burgundy, the Loire and the Jura — each chosen the same morning as the menu it accompanies.

The Rare Cellar

Bottles that rarely leave the vault. Vintage Krug, old Chave Hermitage, and a 1989 d'Yquem poured by the thimble.

The Sans-Alcool

House-made infusions, pressed juices and clarified teas, composed with the same gravity as the wines they stand beside.

The House

Lit only by
flame and intention

A 1687 townhouse, restored over four years. Oak, marble, beeswax candles — and the quiet confidence of a room that has nothing to prove.
